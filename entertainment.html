Twelve angry men:

12 Angry Men, American courtroom film drama, released in 1957, considered a classic of the genre. It marked the feature-film directorial debut of Sidney Lumet. The film was adapted from a 1954 television play that aired on the series Studio One. It centres on the deliberations of 12 jurors serving on a murder case. Eleven jurors vote for a quick conviction, but one holdout (played by Henry Fonda) painstakingly tries to convince the others that the accused may be innocent, despite overwhelming evidence to the contrary.

Travel: Visiting Morocco

To travel to Morocco is to experience one of the true jewels of North Africa. It is a land of profound historical and cultural importance. A land of endless diversity and constant surprises, a land of Islamic imperial grandeur. Morocco is a country where the past and present live together in fascinating harmony. You will find it a country both enchantingly exotic, yet comfortingly familiar.

Documentary: Planet Earth

Planet Earth, narrated by Sir David Attenborough, is a groundbreaking documentary series that premiered in 2006. It explores Earth's diverse ecosystems through stunning visuals and innovative filming techniques, showcasing wildlife and their habitats across the globe. The series emphasizes the fragility of nature and the impact of human activity on the environment, aiming to inspire conservation efforts and a deeper appreciation for the planet's biodiversity
